# It All Started

Sometime I can't recall, I got my first high-end CPU — an i7 13700k. Then, as everyone knows, the ||{[(13th/14th gen CPU degradation scandal)]} aka "silicon degradation" — a fatal bug in the microcode algorithm caused CPUs to forcibly sustain high-frequency, high-voltage states even when temperatures exceeded thresholds, with some voltages spiking to 1.8V| hit the scene. Unfortunately, I was one of the affected.

On March 7, 2026, I bought my first A770 8G Flux OC. It looked great, and I originally planned to use it for OBS recording and streaming. But after installing drivers, even the lightest load caused 100% driver crashes and blue screens. After a restart, the card was 100% lost every time. I searched the entire internet — people said driver version 6299 was stable, so I tried that, but it still blue-screened. The vendor tested Timespy and everything else with no issues. I ended up reselling it. No idea how it's doing now.

And then today (2026.03.30), I just couldn't take it anymore. I absolutely have to give Intel a piece of my mind.

# Intel's Glorious Track Record

## 2005

In 2005, Intel nearly acquired NVIDIA. The CEO at the time offered 20 billion USD to buy NVIDIA, but the board rejected it due to the high price and uncertainty about GPU development. Imagine if the acquisition had gone through — AMD probably wouldn't exist today.

## 2006

Steve Jobs, co-founder of Apple, approached Intel's CEO to produce processors for the iPhone, but the CEO rejected him because Apple's offer was below Intel's cost expectation. Shortly after, iPhone shipment volumes far exceeded personal computers. ||{[(Intel missed the entire mobile internet wave)]}Plot twist: In 2019, Apple acquired Intel's smartphone modem business — roughly 2000+ employees and 17K+ patents. 🤣|.

## 2010–2016

||{[(AMD FX — Bulldozer series — failed to meet expectations)]}High frequency, low performance, power consumption went through the roof|. It created a legend: i3 beats all, i5 beats all, i7 crushes everything. Then ~~the牙膏厂 (Incremental Upgrade Factory)~~ Intel developed the habit of "tick-tock" incremental improvements. Gen 4–7 Core series averaged only 5% improvement. In 2014, they started patching and tweaking. The planned 10nm process was delayed indefinitely, and 14nm was used for 7 years.

## 2017

OpenAI — the company behind ChatGPT — needs no introduction. In 2017, Intel had the opportunity to acquire 15% of OpenAI's shares for 1 billion USD (plus hardware at cost plus another 15%), becoming the dominant investor. But the CEO at the time thought generative AI wouldn't make waves in the short term and passed. Just 5 years later, in late 2022, ChatGPT officially launched and gained 1 million users in 5 days, 100 million monthly active users in 2 months.

## 2021

The big.LITTLE architecture launched. Marketing called it power-saving. P+E core combos on desktops, supposedly so background tasks wouldn't interfere with foreground ones. But Windows 10 doesn't support big.LITTLE scheduling, and Windows 11 is still a mess. Some games even steal E-cores. Can I ask — why am I thinking about power savings on a desktop?

## 2022–2024

In 2022, Intel finally remembered being beaten by NVIDIA and AMD, and launched Arc discrete GPUs to fight back. By 2024, Intel's gaming GPU market share dropped to 0% 🤣. ||{[(2025 confirmed discontinuation of A770 and A750.)]}Reference designs no longer produced. Bulk orders still being made; final shipment date around May 20, 2026|. Any waves? Well, ||{[(Arc broke 1% market share in Q3 2025!)]}Reason: Low-price inventory clearance XD|.

## 2024

Many users reported that i9 and i7 CPUs were crashing games or blue-screening during gameplay. People thought it was the GPU — NVIDIA took some of the blame for a while. ||{[(Later confirmed to be Intel's own problem)]}Intel's internal document etvb — microcode algorithm bug (mentioned above). Under prolonged high-temperature, high-voltage conditions, transistors degrade and CPU is permanently damaged|. Intel claimed a 2% failure rate. In my personal opinion, it's over 70%. I even saw reports of i3 silicon degradation on Bilibili 😂. They also acknowledged oxidation issues with copper through-holes on 13th gen. I happened to be one of the unlucky ones XD.

Next came the Ultra series Core processors. Hyperthreading removed. 性能倒退. No, no — so humorous 🤣. Gaming performance can't even match the previous gen 149K XD. ||{[(Memory latency exploded)]}Chiplet design resulted in latency of 80–120ns, 14th gen was only ~50|. ||{[(PCIe 5.0 SSD speeds slowed down)]}Gen5 ports from 21–24 have high latency, NVMe performance dropped 16%|. Of course, there are some upsides — lower gaming power consumption, lower temperatures. But I'm on a desktop — why would I care about power and cooling? XD.
